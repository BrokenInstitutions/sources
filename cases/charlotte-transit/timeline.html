Charlotte Transit Case Timeline

This timeline chronicles the key events from January through August 2025, documenting the sequence of decisions, legal proceedings, and circumstances that led to the tragic incident. All dates and events are verified through official court records, police reports, and federal documentation.

2013-2024
Pattern of Violence Emerges
Decarlos Brown Jr. accumulates 14 arrests over 11 years including multiple assault charges, battery incidents, weapons violations, and documented threats of violence. Court records show a consistent pattern of escalating behavior.
Court Records
2020-2024
Mental Health Crisis Documented
Medical records document Brown's diagnosis with schizophrenia, paranoid delusions about government surveillance, and increasing disconnection from reality. Family members repeatedly seek help through official channels.
Medical Records
January 19, 2025
Magistrate Teresa Stokes' Release Decision
Following Brown's arrest for misusing emergency services, Magistrate Stokes reviews his extensive criminal history and current mental health crisis. Despite clear public safety concerns, she releases him on a "written promise to appear."
Magistrate Records
February-July 2025
System Delays and Inaction
For six months, Brown remains free in the community. Court-ordered mental health evaluations are delayed, rescheduled, and postponed. Multiple warnings from family and community members fail to trigger intervention.
Court Scheduling
July 28, 2025
Competency Evaluation Ordered
Chief District Judge Roy Wiggins finally orders a comprehensive competency evaluation for Brown. However, the evaluation process is allowed to extend indefinitely while Brown remains free in the community.
Judicial Order
August 22, 2025
Iryna Zarutska Murdered
Brown boards Charlotte Area Transit System light rail and fatally stabs 23-year-old Ukrainian refugee Iryna Zarutska. She had survived Russian bombing campaigns in her homeland, only to die from failures in American institutional safety systems.
Police Investigation
August 23, 2025
Rapid System Response
Within 24 hours of the murder, Brown is transferred to Central Regional Hospital-Butner for psychiatric evaluation. The competency assessment that couldn't be completed in eight months is expedited and completed within days.
Hospital Transfer
September 2025
Federal Prosecution Begins
The U.S. Department of Justice files federal charges against Brown for the transit system murder. Federal prosecutors move swiftly to ensure the case proceeds through the justice system without further delays.
Federal Court
