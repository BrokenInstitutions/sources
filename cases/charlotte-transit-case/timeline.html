Charlotte Transit Case Timeline

How judicial failures armed a killer

2013-2024
Decarlos Brown's Criminal History
Brown accumulates 14 arrests over 11 years including assault, battery, weapons charges, and violent threats. Each time, judges prioritize progressive rehabilitation over public safety, releasing him back to the streets.
Criminal Records
January 19, 2025
Magistrate Teresa Stokes' Fatal Decision
Despite Brown's violent history and documented schizophrenia with paranoid delusions about government implants, Magistrate Stokes releases him on a "written promise to appear" after his arrest for misusing 911 services.
Court Records
July 28, 2025
Judge Wiggins Orders Competency Evaluation
Chief District Judge Roy Wiggins finally orders a competency evaluation for Brown, but allows "procedural delays" to stretch the process for months while Brown remains free to harm innocent people.
Court Documents
August 22, 2025
Iryna Zarutska Murdered
Brown boards a Charlotte transit train and murders 23-year-old Ukrainian refugee Iryna Zarutska. She had survived Russian bombardment in Ukraine only to be killed by American judicial negligence. Eight months after Stokes' release decision.
Police Report
August 23, 2025
Sudden System Efficiency
Within 24 hours of the murder, Brown is transferred to Central Regional Hospital-Butner. The same competency evaluation that couldn't be completed in 8 months is suddenly expedited and finished within days.
Hospital Records
September 2025
Federal Charges Filed
Brown faces federal charges for the murder. The system that moved at glacial pace before the killing suddenly discovers remarkable efficiency and resources after an innocent person dies.
Federal Court
Present Day
No Judicial Accountability
Neither Magistrate Stokes nor Judge Wiggins face any consequences for their decisions that enabled Iryna's murder. Judicial immunity protects officials whose negligence arms killers while families suffer without justice.
