Standards and Processes for Institutional Accountability Investigations

Source Verification Standards

Source Reliability Hierarchy

Tier 1: Primary Official Sources
  • Government press releases and official statements
  • Court documents and legal filings
  • Law enforcement reports and records
  • Official institutional documents (FOIA responses)
Tier 2: Verified Secondary Sources
  • Major news organizations with editorial standards
  • Local media with direct source access
  • Academic research and statistical databases
  • Professional journalism investigations
Tier 3: Supporting Context Sources
  • Social media posts (when directly relevant)
  • Public statements and interviews
  • Opinion pieces with factual content
  • Background and contextual information

Case Documentation Protocol

1. Source Collection Phase

Comprehensive gathering of all available primary sources, including government documents, court records, and official statements. We prioritize Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) requests and public records access.

2. Timeline Development

Creation of detailed chronological sequences based on verified dates and events. All timeline entries include source attribution and verification status.

3. Pattern Analysis

Systematic examination of institutional decisions, policy applications, and procedural outcomes to identify recurring themes and systemic issues.

4. Comparative Research

International and jurisdictional comparisons to provide context and identify alternative approaches that have proven more effective.

5. Reform Recommendations

Development of evidence-based policy suggestions grounded in successful implementations from other jurisdictions and academic research.

Quality Control Standards

Fact Verification

  • Minimum two independent source confirmation
  • Cross-reference dates, names, and events
  • Direct citation of primary sources when possible
  • Clear notation when claims cannot be independently verified

Legal Compliance

  • Adherence to defamation and privacy laws
  • Focus on public officials and public actions
  • Distinction between factual reporting and opinion
  • Respect for ongoing legal proceedings

Ethical Standards

  • Victim sensitivity and family respect
  • Avoidance of sensationalism
  • Focus on institutional rather than personal failures
  • Commitment to accuracy over speed

Transparency Requirements

  • Complete source documentation
  • Clear methodology explanation
  • Acknowledgment of limitations
  • Regular updates when new information emerges

International Comparison Framework

Our international comparisons follow established academic and policy research methodologies:

  • Statistical Sources: UN Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC), World Health Organization (WHO), and national statistical agencies
  • Policy Analysis: Examination of legal frameworks, judicial procedures, and enforcement mechanisms
  • Outcome Measurement: Focus on public safety outcomes, recidivism rates, and institutional effectiveness
  • Contextual Factors: Consideration of cultural, economic, and social differences when making comparisons

Source Verification Process

Every piece of information undergoes our systematic verification protocol:

  1. Primary Source Identification: Locate original documents or official statements
  2. Cross-Reference Verification: Confirm information through multiple independent sources
  3. Date and Timeline Confirmation: Verify chronological accuracy through official records
  4. Context Validation: Ensure information is presented with appropriate context and background
  5. Update Monitoring: Track developments and update information as new sources become available

Limitations and Transparency

We acknowledge the limitations of our methodology and maintain transparency about constraints:

  • Source Availability: Some official documents may be sealed or unavailable
  • Jurisdiction Differences: Legal and procedural variations across jurisdictions
  • Timeline Constraints: Ongoing cases may limit available information
  • Resource Limitations: Independent research constraints on investigation scope

Commitment to Accuracy

We prioritize accuracy over speed and are committed to correcting any errors that come to light. Our methodology emphasizes thorough verification and transparent documentation to maintain the highest standards of investigative journalism.

Continuous Improvement

Our methodology evolves based on:

  • Feedback from legal and journalism professionals
  • New developments in investigative techniques
  • Lessons learned from completed investigations
  • Changes in public records access and legal frameworks

We remain committed to maintaining and improving our standards to serve the public interest through accurate, transparent, and accountable institutional reporting.
